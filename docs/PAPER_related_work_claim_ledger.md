## Evidence rule

- **direct:** the cited source explicitly states the property used in the manuscript.
- **partial:** the source supports part of the statement, while the comparison, absence claim, or synthesis is the present paper's interpretation.
- **unsupported:** the cited source does not support the wording as written.
- An absence in the audited papers is not evidence that no other work exists. The ledger therefore replaces field-wide novelty claims with bounded statements such as “among the cited works” or “the narrower question addressed here.”
- Page locators below refer to the public PDF page count unless published pagination is stated separately.

## Resulting novelty boundary

The audited corpus supports the following bounded position:

1. Governed writes, state-transition policies, versioned belief revision, approval-before-recall, ratified institutional selection, origin-bound non-malleable action authority, dependency-aware cascade repair, and multi-principal governance evaluation are prior art.
2. The terms **laundering** and **ratification** are also prior art in relevant neighboring senses. This paper therefore claims neither term in the abstract nor ratification-gated governance in general.
3. The paper's narrower proposed contribution is the combined contract in which an exact capability-scoped `Grant` is the sole positive root of persistent declaration authority; a named checkout separates grant prefix from reduction frontier; and authority-sensitive use requires a surviving proof instance through projection or computation.
4. This ledger does not establish that no uncited work has proposed the same combination. Publication-stage novelty claims require a broader systematic search and independent review.
